Birds Eye Tour of Oregon Coast

Chase Krogh

01 Coos Bay

Founded in 1891, Coos Bay is a town of about sixteen thousand residents. The city is about 16 square miles and is covered with many hills and small bodies of water including Upeer Pony Creek Reservoir and Empire Lakes.

02 Florence

The land we now call Florence was once inhabited by the Siuslaw Nation of Native Peoples. Today it is home to a population of roughly nine thousand people. There is active debate to this day regarding how Florence earned its name. Some say it was named after A.B. Florence, a US congressional represenative who served in Lane County from 1858 to 1860. Others claim it was named after The SS Siuslaw, a French Naval Vessel that wrecked at the mouth of the Siuslaw river (where Florence now sits) in 1875.

03 Yachats

The land we now call Yachats was inhabited by Siletz, Coos and Lower Umqua peoples for over 1,500 years before European colonizers arrived. Originially the city was called 'Oceanview' but since this name was becoming increasingly common along coastal communities, locals changed it to 'Yachats' in 1917, which means 'Dark Water at the Foot of the Mountain' in Siletz. (I highly recommend Yachats Brewery for anyone passing through town!)

04 Waldport

Waldport was founded by German settlers in the late 1870s, getting its name from the German word 'Wald' which means forest and 'Port' for its location. The iconic Alsea Bay Bridge was first built in 1936 but was rebuilt more recently in 1991.

05 Newport

Newport was founded in 1882 and was named after Newport in Rhode Island. With a population of over ten thousand residents it is the largest town in Lincoln County, Oregon. Yacona Native peoples inhabited the land for over three thousand years before it was colonized.
